Explore Planets

Learn fascinating facts about planets, play a fun quiz, and challenge yourself!

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has extremely hot days and freezing nights.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with a thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with 70% of its surface covered by water.

Mars

Mars, known as the Red Planet, has the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet, famous for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm raging for centuries.

Saturn

Saturn is well known for its stunning rings, made of ice and rock particles.

Uranus

Uranus rotates on its side and has a unique blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its intense winds and dark storms.

Pluto

Though no longer classified as a planet, Pluto remains a beloved member of our solar system.
