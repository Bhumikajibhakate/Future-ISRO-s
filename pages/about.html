About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was established in 1969 with the goal of harnessing space technology for India's national development. Over the decades, ISRO has made remarkable progress in space exploration, launching numerous satellites and missions that have placed India among the leading space-faring nations in the world.

ISRO's mission is to provide the nation with reliable and cost-effective space-based services and to achieve self-reliance in space technology. Notable achievements include the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), Chandrayaan missions, and the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), among others.
